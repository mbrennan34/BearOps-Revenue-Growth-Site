Vision & Strategy

Ownership, Accountability & High-Performance Teams

Turn your EOS Accountability Chart, People Analyzer, L10 cadence and Issues list into a real operating system for ownership across your revenue team – not just a set of slides.

The Problem: By the time you’re at $3–10M ARR, you’ve bolted on leaders, pods and “special projects” faster than you’ve clarified who actually owns what. Titles look good on the org chart, but day-to-day decisions loop, issues resurface and founders get dragged back into people decisions they thought they’d delegated.

Why This Matters Now

At the founder-led stage, the CEO is the de facto owner of everything. That works at 5–10 people. Once you hit 25–80 people, it breaks completely. You hire smart people who end up stepping on each other’s toes – not because they’re incompetent, but because no one has clearly defined who owns what.

The result? Decisions that should take days take weeks. The same issues reappear in L10. “Shadow owners” quietly carry the load without authority. High performers get frustrated; average performers hide in the grey areas. “Everyone owns revenue” becomes “no one owns revenue.”

Component 3 connects three critical layers of your People Operating System:

  • Seats & Structure: Functions → seats → people, with one owner per outcome.
  • Fit & Performance: Values + GWC (Gets it, Wants it, Capacity) via the People Analyzer.
  • Cadence & Culture: Issues, IDS and L10 discipline, backed by a culture & accountability heatmap so you can see if it’s actually working.

How This Works in Practice

Real Client Example

Series A SaaS Company, $5M ARR: VP Sales and VP Marketing were constantly fighting about lead quality. Marketing said “we’re hitting lead volume targets” while Sales said “these leads are garbage.” The CEO spent 40% of their time mediating.

The Root Issue: No one owned “qualified pipeline.” Marketing owned lead volume. Sales owned closed-won revenue. The gap between them was nobody’s problem – which meant it was everyone’s problem.

After the Ownership & Accountability Framework:

  • Created a “Director of Revenue Operations” role (promoted from within) who owned qualified pipeline.
  • Marketing’s scorecard: MQLs that convert to SQLs at 35%+ rate (not just volume).
  • Sales Development’s scorecard: SQLs that reach discovery stage at 60%+ rate.
  • Weekly L10s with clear metrics: everyone sees the funnel health, no more finger-pointing.

Result: Lead-to-SQL conversion improved from 18% to 38% in two quarters. CEO time spent mediating conflicts dropped to near zero. The team actually liked each other again.
