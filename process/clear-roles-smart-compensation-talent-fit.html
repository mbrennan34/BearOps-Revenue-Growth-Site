Process & Execution

Clear Roles, Smart Compensation & Rigorous Talent Fit

Who does what, how to hire for fit, and how managers coach systematically instead of firefighting.

The Problem: You hired smart people who keep stepping on each other's toes. Your SDR team doesn't know if they report to Sales or Marketing. AEs don't know when to loop in Solutions Engineering. Managers spend all their time in deal reviews, zero time actually developing reps.

Why This Matters

At 3-5 reps, everyone does everything. At 15+ reps, that chaos breaks. You need clear lanes: Who qualifies? Who demos? Who closes? Who owns renewal? Without role clarity, you get turf wars, duplicated effort, and deals falling through cracks.

Worse: You're hiring based on resumes, not fit. That shiny VP Sales from [Big Tech Co] doesn't know how to operate in your chaotic, under-resourced startup environment. Three months in, they're struggling and you've burned $150K.

What We Build Together

Role Architecture

Clear definitions: SDR vs BDR, AE vs Sr AE, CSM vs AM. Swim lanes, handoff points, decision rights for each role.

Talent Scorecards

What actually predicts success? Not "5 years of SaaS sales"—competencies, behaviors, values that matter for YOUR business.

Compensation Philosophy

OTE structure, accelerators, when to use SPIFs. Fair, motivating comp that aligns to business outcomes, not just activity.

Coaching Infrastructure

1:1 frameworks, deal review cadence, skills development plans. Turn managers into coaches, not just deal inspectors.

What You'll Have After 4 Weeks

Role Definition Library: Job descriptions that actually describe success criteria, not just tasks
Hiring Scorecards: Structured interview guides and scorecards that reduce bias, improve hire quality
Compensation Framework: Salary bands, OTE splits, commission plans aligned to your unit economics

Real Client Example

Series A PLG SaaS, $6M ARR:

Had 8 AEs but couldn't define the difference between an AE and a Sr AE. Everyone had same quota ($500K), same comp plan, same territory model. Result: Top 2 reps hit 150% of quota, bottom 4 hit 30%. Massive comp disparity, resentment, high turnover.

After Roles & Talent Workshop:

  • Created tiered roles: Jr AE ($300K quota, inbound leads), AE ($500K quota, mixed), Sr AE ($750K quota, strategic accounts)
  • Built competency model: What skills needed for each tier? Created assessment framework
  • Right-sized team: 3 Jr AEs, 4 AEs, 1 Sr AE based on actual skill levels and deal complexity
  • Manager training: Taught sales managers how to coach to competency gaps, not just inspect deals
  • Result: Quota attainment spread narrowed dramatically—70% of team now at 80%+ vs 25% before

Bonus: Retention improved because people had clear career paths and felt fairly compensated for their level.
