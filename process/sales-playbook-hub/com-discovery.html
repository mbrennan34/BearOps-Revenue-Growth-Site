üìã What are Value Drivers?

  • Things that the prospect is likely proactively looking for or needing.
  • Generally, revenue, cost, or risk related.
  • Top-of-mind customer challenges that exist even if your company doesn't.
  • Could cause a buyer to re-allocate discretionary funds.
  • Supports a value-based sales track (mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive).

We explain value drivers in the following structure:

  1. The Before Scenario and the Negative Consequences of it.
  2. The After Scenario and the Positive Business Outcomes from it.
  3. The Required Capabilities and the Metrics to prove success.
  4. How Your Company Does It and How You Do It Better.
  5. Proof Points like customer references and case studies.
  6. Discovery Questions to determine what's driving the customer's decision-making.

üéØ What are Differentiators?

Differentiators are what make people want to buy from us. They influence a technical buyer's decision criteria and the Required Capabilities. Each Differentiator contains a Description, Customer Value, Defensibility, and Trap-Setting Questions.

There are 3 types of differentiators:

  • Unique: Not available from any other vendor. Leading products or services that only your company provides.
  • Comparative: Superior attributes when compared to other companies, often feature or service based.
  • Holistic: Attributes of your company that would make a customer feel good about doing business with you.

We explain the Differentiators using:

  1. The Differentiator itself (e.g., Data Consolidation).
  2. The value to the customer (revenue, cost, and/or risk).
  3. The defensibility, similar to Proof Points, from our customer's point of view.
  4. Trap-Setting Questions; the topics you'll want to bring up to drive prospects to the Differentiators.
