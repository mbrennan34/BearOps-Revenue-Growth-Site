People & Process

Roles, Talent Fit & Leadership Cadence

Designing the right org structure, hiring profile, and leadership rhythm so your managers stop firefighting and start consistently improving performance.

The Problem: As you scale, you add titles faster than clarity. AEs, SDRs, AMs and CSMs have overlapping responsibilities. Managers run 1:1s and team meetings, but there’s no consistent structure, and coaching is ad hoc at best.

Why This Matters

Revenue is now a team sport. If roles aren’t clearly defined and your leadership cadence isn’t tight, you end up paying for capacity you never fully unlock. Underperformers linger, top performers carry the load, and you as CEO get dragged into people issues you shouldn’t be owning.

What We Build Together

Org Design & Role Definitions

Clear swimlanes for SDR, AE, AM, CSM, RevOps and Marketing—who owns what, and how they interact.

Talent Profiles & Scorecards

Role scorecards with must-haves, nice-to-haves, and measurable outcomes that hiring and performance reviews are anchored to.

Leadership Cadence

Standardised 1:1s, pipeline reviews, and team meetings so managers spend time on coaching, not random status updates.

Performance Management Framework

Clear expectations, simple PIP structure, and timelines so you can move fast on both promotion and exit decisions.

What You’ll Have After 3 Weeks

Revenue Org Blueprint A one-page view of today’s org plus “next org” at the next ARR milestone, so you’re not hiring in a vacuum.
Role Scorecards Documented outputs and competencies for each key GTM role, used across hiring, onboarding and reviews.
Manager Playbook Standard templates for 1:1s, forecast reviews and coaching conversations—aligned across all revenue leaders.
Performance & Escalation Paths Simple guidelines for when to coach, when to restructure, and when to exit—reducing emotional decision-making.

How This Works in Practice

Week 1: Org Reality & Gaps

  • Review current org, responsibilities and performance spread
  • Identify overlapping responsibilities and ownership gaps
  • Understand where managers spend their time today

Week 2: Design & Alignment

  • Draft future-state org and role definitions
  • Create role scorecards and hiring profiles
  • Design leadership cadence and standard agendas

Week 3: Rollout & Coaching

  • Walk leaders through new cadence and expectations
  • Run first “model” meetings together (pipeline, 1:1s)
  • Hand over templates and escalation playbook
