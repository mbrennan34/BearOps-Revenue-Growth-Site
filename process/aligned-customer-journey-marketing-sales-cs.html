Aligned Customer Journey Across Marketing, Sales & CS

Fair territories, whitespace analysis, and data-driven forecasting that actually predicts revenue.

The Problem: Your territories are "whoever joined first gets the best accounts." Forecasts are Excel guesswork. You don't know which deals will actually close until they close (or don't). Reps fight over accounts instead of selling.

Why This Matters

Bad territory design kills productivity. Your best rep has all the enterprise logos while a newer rep has scraps. Or worse—accounts split randomly with no logic, causing customer confusion and internal conflict.

Meanwhile, your forecast is a bottom-up roll-up of rep guesses. Everyone sandbags or over-commits. You tell the board "$2.5M this quarter" with 60% confidence. That's not forecasting—that's hoping with spreadsheets.

What We Build Together

Territory Model

Fair distribution based on potential, not seniority. Geographic, vertical, account-based—whatever fits your GTM.

Account Segmentation

Which accounts are strategic? Which are growth? Which are maintain? Clear prioritization so reps focus right.

Forecasting Methodology

Stage-based probability, deal inspection criteria, historical close rate analysis. Math, not hope.

Revenue Analytics Dashboard

Pipeline coverage, velocity trends, conversion rates by stage. See problems 6 weeks before they hit the number.

What You'll Have After 4 Weeks

Territory Assignment Model: Clear criteria for how accounts get assigned, rebalancing triggers
Account Tiering Framework: A/B/C segmentation with coverage models (how much attention each tier gets)
Forecast Model: Stage probabilities, close rate assumptions, pipeline coverage requirements
Analytics Dashboard: Real-time pipeline health, deal velocity, conversion funnel, rep performance
Quarterly Territory Review Process: When to rebalance, how to handle disputes, growth vs fairness trade-offs

Real Client Example

Series B SaaS, $14M ARR, 12 AEs:

Territories were "first come, first served." Top 2 reps had all the Fortune 1000 accounts from early wins. Newer reps had SMB accounts with 1/3 the deal size. Predictably, top reps hit 180% quota, newer reps hit 40%. Comp disparity caused 3 resignations in 6 months.

After Territory & Forecasting Workshop:

  • Analyzed all accounts by revenue potential, product fit, competitive landscape
  • Created balanced territories: Each rep got mix of enterprise, mid-market, growth accounts
  • Grandfather clause: Existing relationships stayed, but new logo assignments followed new model
  • Built forecast model: Stage probability based on 2 years of historical data, not gut feel
  • Result: Quota attainment spread narrowed from 140 points to 60 points. Forecast accuracy went 53% → 89%

CEO's take: "For the first time, I can tell the board our quarterly number with confidence. And my team stopped fighting over accounts—they're selling."
