The Metrics That Matter

Leading Indicators (Predict Future Revenue)

  • Pipeline Coverage: Pipeline value ÷ quota (need 3-4x for healthy forecast)
  • Pipeline Build Rate: New pipeline added per week (must exceed closed-won + closed-lost)
  • Deal Velocity: Average days in each stage (slowing = problem)
  • Stage Conversion Rates: Discovery → Demo → Proposal → Close (track trends)

Lag Indicators (Confirm What Happened)

  • Bookings vs Quota: Are we on track this quarter?
  • Win Rate: Closed-won ÷ total opportunities (by segment, competitor, source)
  • Sales Cycle Length: First touch → closed-won (by deal size)
  • Quota Attainment Distribution: What % of reps hit 80%+?

Efficiency Metrics (Cost to Acquire)

  • CAC Payback Period: Months to recover acquisition cost
  • CAC Ratio: New ARR ÷ S&M spend (should be 1.0+ by Series B)
  • Sales Efficiency: Revenue per rep, deals per rep, activities required per deal

Tool Selection

CRM Native: Salesforce Reports/Dashboards or HubSpot Reporting—good for simple needs, limited customization.

Lightweight BI: Metabase (open source), Google Data Studio (free)—great for startups, easy to set up.

Enterprise BI: Tableau, Looker, Power BI—powerful, expensive, worth it at scale ($20M+ ARR).

Our recommendation: For Series A/B under $15M ARR, start with CRM native reporting or Metabase. Don't over-invest in BI infrastructure until you have clean data and know what questions you need answered.
