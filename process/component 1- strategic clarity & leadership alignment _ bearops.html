10-Year Target (BHAG)

Your Big Hairy Audacious Goal - a specific, measurable target you'll achieve in 10 years. Should be ambitious (50-70% confidence) yet achievable.

Jim Collins BHAG Framework

A BHAG should be:

  • Specific: You'll know when you achieve it
  • Measurable: Numbers you can track
  • Audacious: 50-70% confidence (not 95%)
  • Emotional: Gets you out of bed in the morning
  • Aligned: Supports your Core Purpose

Niche & Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Define your ideal customer with precision using a data-driven, three-pillar approach. Companies with well-defined ICPs achieve 68% higher win rates and 30-40% shorter sales cycles.

üí° Critical Distinction

Your ICP describes who you WANT more of (top 10-20% of customers), not just who you HAVE. Base it on your most successful customers, not your average customers.

ü§ñ AI ICP Analyzer

I'll analyze your ICP across three dimensions: qualitative insights, quantitative patterns, and market validation. Then suggest refinements based on win rates, LTV, and competitive positioning.

Step 1: Identify Your Top 10 Customers

Start with data. Who are your most successful customers based on: highest LTV, longest retention, fastest sales cycle, highest NPS, most expansion revenue, and best advocacy?

Pro Tip: Look for patterns across your top 10. What do they have in common that your struggling customers don't?

Step 2: Define ICP Attributes

Use the three-pillar framework: firmographics (company data), behavioral indicators (how they buy/use), and environmental factors (context/triggers).

üìä Firmographic Attributes

Avoid broad categories. "B2B SaaS" is better than "Software". "PE-backed HR Tech" is even better.

üéØ Behavioral & Use Case Attributes

Focus on the problem, not your solution. What keeps them up at night?

üåç Environmental & Contextual Factors

What tech do they use that makes them a good fit? Any dependencies or integrations?

Step 3: Tier Your ICP

Not all ideal customers are equal. Create tiers to prioritize resources effectively.

1

Tier 1: Bull's Eye

Perfect fit on ALL criteria. Expected win rate: 40-60%.

  • All-hands personalized approach
  • Executive engagement
  • Custom proposals/demos
  • Highest priority for CS
2

Tier 2: Good Fit

Meet most criteria. Expected win rate: 25-40%.

  • Standard sales process
  • Marketing nurture campaigns
  • Template-based approach
  • Standard CS touchpoints
3

Tier 3: Acceptable

Meet minimum criteria. Expected win rate: 15-25%.

  • Low-touch/self-serve
  • Automated marketing
  • Lower priority
  • Qualify carefully

Step 4: Define Disqualification Criteria

Equally important: Who should you NOT pursue? Saying "no" to bad fits is as important as saying "yes" to good ones.

üìä How We'll Measure ICP Effectiveness

Track these metrics quarterly to validate your ICP:

  • Win Rate by Tier: Tier 1 should be 40%+, Tier 2: 25-40%, Tier 3: 15-25%
  • Sales Cycle by Tier: Tier 1 should be 30-40% shorter than average
  • LTV:CAC by Tier: Tier 1 should be 3:1 or higher
  • Churn Rate by Tier: Tier 1 should be <10% annually
  • Pipeline Quality: % of pipeline that's Tier 1 or 2 (target: 70%+)
